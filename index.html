Why Volta?

A 'Volta' is a poetic device in English which represents a turn in thought, or a change in idea. I decided to incorporate this into by business name as I believe private tutoring and teaching can be a pivotal step into the success of a student (be it through weekly classes or workshops) and hopefully create a 'Volta' towards the desired exam results.

TUTORING SESSIONS

I offer tutoring sessions for homework help, mock and exam preparation, and general tuition. Following the National curriculum syllabus and exam board specifications, I ensure that your child receives the most tailored teaching for their individual success.

Sessions are an hour long either once or twice weekly, whenever is best suited to you. I align my sessions with the student's school term dates with the option for half term and holiday sessions to bridge the gaps of learning breaks. Currently sessions are ran virtually due to their success during the pandemic. Students access their session via Skype or Facetime and all resources are sent via email for access on their device and shared on screen.

Payments are made via bank transfer or standing order paid every half term in advance. Students may attend one trial session at the same hourly rate before committing to a half term payment. Please see below for the pricing details. I also offer one-off sessions to focus on a particular areas of weakness if the student does not need a full half term of sessions but just a little bit of help.

WHAT I OFFER


SINGING LESSONS

In 1-1 and group singing and choral lessons, learners can work on pop and musical theatre repertoire focussing on enhancing performance skills and improving technique. Lessons can work towards performances or be in line with exam board syllabus' to help prepare students for singing exams such as LCM, Trinity and ABRSM.

ACTING LESSONS

Acting lessons can take many different forms depending on the desired outcome. Lessons can be used to build confidence in performing and gain valuable skills through the use of monologues, duologues or ensemble work. Sessions can be used for audition prep for drama schools or for exams such as LAMDA or RADA

DANCE LESSONS

Dance lessons can specialise in Musical Theatre, Jazz, Tap, Ballet, Contemporary and Commercial styles. Lessons will focus on the improvement of technique and the joy that connecting with our body and expression can have. Lessons can help aid in students in preparation for graded exams such as ISTD and RAD

MUSICAL THEATRE CLASSES

Musical Theatre workshops and classes can be an incredible way to bring together all three disciplines of singing, acting and dancing. Working with musical theatre repertoire, choreography and scenes, students can work on combining theatrical elements in a fun and exciting medium.

MY PERFORMING AND TEACHING EXPERIENCE

I have always loved musical theatre and took part in a variety of dance, singing and acting classes from a young age and performed regularly at local theatres and in school productions. I learned the RAD (Royal Academy of Dance) ballet syllabus as a child and sat exams in ISTD (Imperial Society of Teachers of Dance) Classical Ballet and Musical Theatre singing with London College of Music.

I am a singing teacher for students aged 4-18 in Saturday performing arts classes focussing on musical theatre and commercial pop. I frequently plan and deliver both bi- weekly, weekly and one off workshops and sessions for primary and secondary pupils in musical theatre skills. Exploring and developing the techniques of acting, singing and dance in a fun and engaging way whilst often preparing for end of term performances.

I coach vocal and physical warmups to train aural and kinaesthetic skills before teaching dynamic dances and songs with prepared vocal parts and harmonies. I adapt the difficulty levels appropriately to accommodate their differing ages, needs and ability with a fun approach. I have also partaken work experience in local theatres, classroom assisting in a local primary school, and a reading mentor for younger students.

I have also had a variety of other roles in the performing arts industry ranging from creative and production team roles such as assistant director, stage management assistance and costume and prop designing and chaperoning. I have also partaken in a work experience week for the LEAP (Learning, Education and participation) team at Chichester festival theatre.
